instructions: how to help build the treasure map

participating in junglegroves is about replacing cheap signals (likes/votes) with costly signals (donations) to reveal true priorities. here is how you can add your priorities to the map.

1. choose your suggestion

it can be anything, or nearly so. maybe you want me to plant more of my ficus opposita x carica hybrids to test for root knot nematode resistance, create a wildlife sanctuary, or even bulldoze the grove for a miniature golf course. censorship is anathema to me, so virtually any critique, idea, or suggestion, including self-promotion, is welcome.

2. make a donation

the amount you donate conveys the strength of your preference. there are two ways to do this:

option a (best method):

to avoid exorbitant transaction fees, please message me directly for my personal paypal address so you can select "friends and family".

you can reach me via:

  • facebook: epiphyte
  • instagram: grows.on.trees
  • tropical fruit forum: epiphyte
  • inaturalist: epiphyte78

option b:

use the standard donate button on this site (fees will apply).

3. leave a note

whether you message me directly or use the donate button, please include the following details so i can properly attribute your contribution:

  • what it's for (e.g., "kwai muk" or "wildlife sanctuary")
  • a link to any page (optional)
  • your username (from tff, inaturalist, etc.)

i will manually update the rankings on the homepage as soon as i get a chance.

important: non-binding rankings

please note that these rankings are non-binding. even though i receive and keep the money, i retain full control over the land. this means that even if "gas station" is ranked #1 with a total donation amount of $1 million, i might decide to continue experimenting with rare fruit trees. however, any entrepreneur who visits the junglegrove homepage wouldn't be able to overlook the considerable demand for a gas station. the purpose of your donation is not to buy a binding mandate, but to help construct a public "treasure map" of society's true priorities. by revealing the actual (relative) demand for things, you help ensure that nobody wastes society's limited resources by "opening a hardware store where nobody buys hardware" (an example from the economist israel kirzner). knowing is half the battle. thank you for helping me fight ignorance (of demand) with your hard-earned money.
